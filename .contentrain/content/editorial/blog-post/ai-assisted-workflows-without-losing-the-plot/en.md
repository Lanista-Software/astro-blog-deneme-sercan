## Speed is not the hard part anymore

Generating a first draft, a first pass of code, or a first structure for a document is no longer the bottleneck it used to be. AI tools compress that step from hours to minutes.

The harder problem is judgment: knowing when the output is actually good, and when it just looks confident.

## The failure mode nobody talks about

Teams that lean on AI without a review discipline start shipping content and code that reads well but drifts from what the product actually needs. The tell is subtle — everything looks polished, but decisions stop being traceable to a real reason.

That's the moment automation stops saving time and starts creating cleanup work downstream.

## What keeps the workflow honest

1. Treat AI output as a draft from a fast junior collaborator, not a finished decision.
2. Keep a human checkpoint at the point where output turns into a public commitment — a merged PR, a published page, a sent email.
3. Log why something was accepted, not just that it was accepted.

Used this way, AI-assisted workflows compound the same way good engineering practices do: each cycle gets faster without getting sloppier.
